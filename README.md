# opengl-forest-survival-game_cse423
Forest Survival is a 3D first-person survival shooter developed using OpenGL for the CSE423 – Computer Graphics course.
Players explore a procedurally generated forest environment while fighting wildlife enemies, managing health, and surviving dynamic weather conditions.

The game features real-time rendering, enemy AI, survival mechanics, and weapon systems to create an engaging survival experience.

🎮 Features

🌲 Procedurally Generated Forest Environment

🔫 First-Person Shooter Mechanics

🌧 Dynamic Weather System (day/night & storm cycle)

❤️ Health & Survival System

🐻 Wildlife Enemy AI

🔫 Weapon Switching System

⚡ Special Super Power Ability

🧭 Collision Detection & Physics

🏆 Survival Scoring System

🎲 Gameplay Tips
🧭 Survival Strategy

Collect food to keep your health above zero.

Avoid red traps that deal 10 damage.

Moving through water slows you down.

🔫 Combat

Rifle: longer range

Pistol: faster firing rate

Manage your ammo carefully and collect ammo crates.

🐾 Enemy Types
Animal	Health	Speed	Damage
🐻 Bear	High	Slow	30
🦌 Deer	Medium	Fast	15
🦊 Fox	Low	Very Fast	10
⚡ Super Power

Can be used once per game session

Best used when low health or surrounded

Press R to restart and regain the super power

🌧 Weather Adaptation

Night reduces visibility

Storm effects change atmosphere

Weather cycles every 10 seconds

🎮 Game Controls
Key	Action
W A S D	Move
Mouse	Aim
Left Click	Shoot
1 / 2	Switch weapons
Space	Jump
R	Restart game
ESC	Exit
⚙️ Technical Details

Language: Python 3.x

Graphics API: OpenGL

Library: GLUT (PyOpenGL)

Rendering: Double-buffered RGB with depth testing

Terrain: Chunk-based procedural generation

Physics: Custom collision detection and jumping system

Enemy AI: Distance-based detection and movement
